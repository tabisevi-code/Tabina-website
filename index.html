My Story

In 2020, online during COVID, a friend told me and said she’d be depressed over a situation. I comforted her—but I also said something I regret: “I don’t think depression is real.”

Only later when, for the first time, I feared I might lose myself to some feelings I couldn’t control, did I grasp how real and serious depression could be. This site is my apology to both my friend and myself, and proof of my commitment to empathy.

Stigma: Why It Hurts

Silence

People hide their pain from friends and family to avoid judgment, thereby losing the chance to get help.

Shame

Phrases like “just get over it” just makes the feelings of loneliness and self-blame much worse.

Barriers

Stigma keeps people from seeking the medical treatment and support communities they need.
