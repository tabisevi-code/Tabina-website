From Dismissal to Empathy: My Reflection

Why I once said “I don’t think depression is real,” and how I learned I was wrong.

In 2020, when most of life moved online, a friend told me she had lost her scholarship and was terrified about what came next. She said she might become depressed. I tried to comfort her—but I also said something I still regret: “I don’t think depression is real.”

I wasn’t super happy growing up; I had my own rough phases. But I didn’t understand how depression could take hold—how it could make basic things hard, or make someone feel like they were losing themselves.

Later, as an adult, I faced situations that scared me. I started thinking, “I hope I don’t get depressed from this.” That’s when I realized: it isn’t about being strong or weak. Sometimes the feelings aren’t in your control. I was afraid of losing myself to them—and that fear taught me how real depression is.

Depression is real. Stigma is harmful. Empathy is a choice we can make every day.

What I wish I’d said instead

  • “I’m here for you. Want to talk?”
  • “I don’t know exactly what you’re going through, but I’ll listen.”
  • “It’s okay to ask for help.”

Why I built this site

Because stigma keeps people quiet and alone. Because the words we choose matter. Because I want to be someone who helps others feel safe to speak and seek support.

If you’re struggling, consider reaching out to someone you trust or a mental health professional in your area. You deserve care.
