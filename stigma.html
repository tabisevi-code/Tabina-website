Understanding Mental Health Stigma

Stigma is a set of negative beliefs that leads to shame, silence, and barriers to care. Changing it starts with how we listen and how we speak.

What is stigma?

It’s the labels and stereotypes that make people feel “weak,” “dramatic,” or “broken” for experiencing mental health challenges. Stigma can show up in jokes, dismissive comments, or the idea that seeking help is failure.

How stigma harms

It silences

People avoid sharing what they’re going through to escape judgment—missing out on connection and care.

It blocks help

Shame and fear can keep people from reaching out to friends, family, or professionals.

Words matter

Instead of

  • “Just get over it.”
  • “You’re overreacting.”
  • “Depression isn’t real.”

Try

  • “I’m here. Do you want to talk?”
  • “Your feelings are valid.”
  • “Would you like help finding support?”

What can help

  • Listen without trying to “fix” everything immediately.
  • Use non-judgmental language; avoid minimizing others’ experiences.
  • Encourage seeking support from trusted people or professionals.
  • Normalize rest and self-care as real needs, not luxuries.
